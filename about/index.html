Who's behind this.

01

Founders

Make It So Camp was created by Igor Schwarzmann and Noah Raford.

Igor Schwarzmann ↗︎

Independent strategist and researcher working at the intersection of culture and technology: cultural and trend research, brand and growth strategy, and AI strategy. He ran a foresight and strategic-design studio out of Berlin, which he sold in 2022. Recent clients include frontier AI labs, luxury fashion houses, and home-appliance makers.

Noah Raford ↗︎

Futurist and strategist. Managing Partner at EMIR, he advises businesses and governments on emerging technologies and foresight. He spent a decade in the Government of Dubai as Futurist-in-Chief — founding executive of the Dubai Future Foundation and the Museum of the Future, and senior advisor to the UAE Prime Minister's Office. He holds a PhD from MIT.

02

Collaborators

Each camp was built with someone on the ground. They brought the room, the cohort, and the institutions behind it.

Joseph Austerweil ↗︎

Cognitive psychologist and machine learning researcher. Senior Researcher at the Henkaku Center and co-founding faculty of Chiba Institute of Technology's new School of Design and Science. He believed in the camp early, helped shape what it became, and brought it to Tokyo.

Ariella Helfgott ↗︎

Co-founder of the SA Futures Agency and Professor at the University of Adelaide. Systems thinker working across complexity science, policy, and institutional change. She opened Adelaide to the camp and had it scheduled within weeks.
