## What is Make It So Camp?

A selective, two-day AI workshop for experienced practitioners. It runs by invitation. The premise is that AI can only help with work you can describe, and that most of your real method has never been written down. The camp is for writing it down, then putting it to work with the tools over two days.

## What does "by invitation" mean?

There is no open sign-up. You request an invitation by email, tell us which camp and what work you would bring, and we reply if there is a fit. The only way in is hello@misocamp.com.

## Who is it for?

Experienced practitioners with real work to bring. The cohort mixes domains on purpose: academic (researchers, teachers, lab leads), creative (strategists, designers, writers, cultural workers), and corporate (product, operations, transformation). The mix is deliberate; the method transfers, and the demos are better for it.

## Do I need to be technical?

No. The line on the site is direct: you do not need to be technical, you need work you care about and the patience to say what you mean. If you would rather wait for a playbook, this is not the place. The people here are writing it.

## What is the thesis behind it?

"You cannot delegate what you cannot articulate." AI needs you to say what you mean: the goal, the constraints, what good looks like. The camp starts from your work, not from the tools.

## What do the two days look like?

- **Before.** We ask everyone beforehand what they are working on and how they work today, so Day 1 starts from what is actually in the room rather than a round of introductions.
- **Day 1 — Articulate.** You work on a real problem you bring, and make the method behind it explicit: the steps, the assumptions, what good looks like. Exercises with people from other fields test whether someone else could follow it.
- **Day 2 — Build and demo.** Alone or in a small cross-domain team, you put the written method to work with the tools and build something real. The day ends in demo rounds; not show-and-tell, but what your way of working made possible.

Times on each camp page are indicative; the exact rhythm may shift.

## What do I bring?

A real problem you are working on. Not a case study, not a sandbox exercise; a live problem where your judgment matters and your method has never been written down.

## What do I leave with?

Three things, as the camp puts it: a written method you can keep using, a working thing you built from it, and a set of people who now know how you think.
