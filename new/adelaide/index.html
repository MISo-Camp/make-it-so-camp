Making AI-enabled work explicit enough to share.

Hosted at Flinders University New Venture Institute on 17–18 September 2026, the Adelaide pilot uses the same Make It So Camp structure: pre-session context, a day of articulation work, and a second day where participants build with others and demo what they made.

Pilot shape

How the pilot runs.

The Adelaide pilot is a co-designed learning instance of the camp. Day 1 makes the cohort's methods explicit; Day 2 turns those methods into collaborative build and demo work shaped around the participants, their work, and the New Venture Institute context.

Before the pilot

An online pre-session roughly one month before the event surfaces what is top of mind for the cohort and gives facilitators a view of the room.

Day 1

Participants work through the four Shifts: framing an ambition, making tacit methods visible, writing them down, and comparing them with others.

Day 2

Teams and solo builders turn the Day 1 captures into rough working artifacts, build with and around each other, critique the work, and demo to each other in smaller rounds.

Curriculum

The four Shifts.

The curriculum is about articulation becoming action. Participants leave with a clearer method, a shared language for improving it, and something concrete to discuss.

01

Welcome + Frame

The positioning shift: purposeful, not faster.

Name an ambition worth building toward, then identify the work that must become explicit enough to support it.

02

Shift 2 · Methods You Can't See

The articulation shift: the bottleneck is articulation, not the tech.

Find the instructions, constraints, and judgment calls that usually stay inside a person's head.

03

Shift 3 · Write It Down, It Compounds

The reuse shift: documented once, reused all day.

Turn the method into a reusable head-start so a second attempt begins with better context.

04

Shift 4 · Explicit = Shareable

The collaboration shift: if it is explicit, someone else can run it.

Compare methods across the room and look for protocols that can be tested, challenged, and improved.
