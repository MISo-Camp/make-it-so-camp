Why articulation

The bottleneck isn't technical.

The people who get the most out of AI are not the most technical. When Claude Code shipped, developers quickly went from using it for code to directing it at everything else. Years of programming had trained them to state goals, constraints, and success criteria precisely. The machine does not guess. You have to say exactly what you mean.

That is the gap this camp works on. Not information: tutorials exist in abundance. The gap is turning years of tacit expertise into something precise enough for a machine to execute and a colleague to challenge. Most knowledge workers have never needed to make their methods explicit. The expertise lives in intuition, pattern recognition, and judgment calls made in milliseconds.

Which is why experienced practitioners have the most to gain here. Twenty years of method is the raw material. The camp is where you write it down, hand it to a machine, and watch what it produces.

The format

Purposeful, not faster.

Make It So Camp has two movements: Day 1 makes your method explicit; Day 2 turns that method into collaborative build work. Participants bring real work, make the hidden method visible, write it down, build something real with other people, and demo it to each other.

01

Welcome + Frame

The positioning shift: purposeful, not faster.

Start from a real ambition rather than a task list. Name what you want to build, not only what you want to accelerate.

02

Shift 2 · Methods You Can't See

The articulation shift: the bottleneck is articulation, not the tech.

Translate tacit judgment into instructions, constraints, and decision logic that another actor can follow.

03

Shift 3 · Write It Down, It Compounds

The reuse shift: documented once, reused all day.

Turn the method into a head-start document so the next build begins from shared context instead of a blank page.

04

Shift 4 · Explicit = Shareable

The collaboration shift: if it is explicit, someone else can run it.

Compare methods with peers, find overlaps, and leave with a protocol that can be challenged and improved.
