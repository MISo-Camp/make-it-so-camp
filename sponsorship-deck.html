02 — The Problem

Everyone is experimenting with AI. Almost no one knows how to talk about what's working.

Across industries, knowledge workers are running private experiments with AI tools. Researchers, strategists, operations leads, creatives — each struggling in isolation. There is no shared vocabulary. No pattern library. No moment where anyone says "here's what I'm learning" in a way that makes sense to people who aren't developers.

03 — The Insight

The bottleneck isn't technical skill.
It's the ability to articulate what you actually want.

Vague
"Help me analyze our customer data"
Articulated
"Identify customers who purchased twice in Q1 but not Q2. Segment by channel. Flag 40%+ drop-off."

The people who succeed with AI aren't the most technical. They're the most precise. Knowledge workers have deep expertise locked in intuition, pattern recognition, and judgment. They've never had to make it explicit. Until now.

04 — The Answer

MISo Camp teaches articulation through building.

A 4-day intensive in Tokyo where ~40 curated participants solve real problems from their own practice using AI. Not a hackathon. Not a lecture series. Participants arrive with a problem, build working tools, and discover that their implicit expertise — once made explicit — becomes reusable, shareable, and teachable to both humans and machines.

05 — How It Works

Three loops turn intuition into method.

1
Build
Solve a real problem from your own practice.
"I have methods I didn't know I had."
2
Reuse
Build bigger, using documented approaches from Loop 1.
"Writing it down made this faster."
3
Share
Collaborate using explicit protocols.
"My methods are teachable."

Theory explains experience — it never precedes it.
